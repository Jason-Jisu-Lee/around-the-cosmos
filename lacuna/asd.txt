Lacuna — Mechanics Overview (v3)

Core Concept
Incremental idle game, primarily active. The player orbits planets around a central evolving object — the Lacuna — accumulating stardust. The center evolves through stages, culminating in a black hole prestige event. Active play accelerates everything. Passive play is viable but softly capped.

Resources

Stardust — primary currency. Buys planets and upgrades.
Dark Matter — prestige currency. Earned during the black hole consumption event.


Stardust Generation (3 Sources)

Cosmic drift — center emits a tiny passive trickle from game start. Enough to purchase Planet 1 within ~45 seconds without any input.
Planets — each completed orbit pays out: planet base value × center pull multiplier
Comets — periodic objects crossing the screen. Tap to collect a stardust burst. Primary active mechanic — reactive, never a grind.


Active vs. Passive Balance — Comet Multiplier

A persistent Comet Multiplier applies to all comet payouts
Starts at 1.0×, caps at 2.0×
Decays slowly over ~20 minutes of inactivity back to 1.0×
Any single comet tap reactivates it to full immediately
Passive players earn base rate. Active players earn base + multiplier
Forgiving by design — returning players recover instantly on first tap


Planet System

Planets orbit the center in a circular arrangement
Each orbit completion triggers a stardust payout
Payout formula: planet base value × center pull multiplier
Cap at 10 planets
Planet 1 is purchased with early cosmic drift stardust at ~45 seconds — first meaningful player action
Planets can be individually upgraded for higher base value


Center Object — The Lacuna
Evolves automatically. No player decision required. Each transition is a visual event + pull multiplier increase.

Pull value — the center's core stat. Visible multiplier shown on the center object (e.g. Gravitational Pull: 1.5×). Every planet orbit payout is multiplied by this value. Higher stage = higher pull. Upgrades can increase it further.

Prestige — Black Hole Consumption Event
Triggers when black hole reaches critical mass.

Black hole pulls all orbiting planets inward one by one (visual sequence)
Each absorbed planet yields dark matter proportional to its upgrade level
Center resets to drifting rock — full arc re-experienced, compressed
All multipliers stack and carry over
Rock → black hole cycle is meaningfully faster each run
Demo ends at black hole stage — further evolution is post-demo content


Upgrade Categories (4 Pillars)
1. Actions

Tap/click-based upgrades. Increases stardust from manual interaction.
2. Comets

Affects comet frequency, burst size, and multiplier behavior.
3. Planets

Orbit payout increases, planet base value, new planet slot unlocks.
4. Lacuna (locked until first prestige)

Gravity and pull upgrades exclusively. Unlocking this signals that prestige changed something meaningful.
Example Lacuna upgrades:

Gravitational Field I/II/III — flat pull multiplier increases
Event Horizon — more dark matter per planet consumed at prestige
Tidal Force — comet payouts scale with current pull value


Solar Events
Periodic events that temporarily spike activity. First fires at ~10 minutes. Rotate every 7–9 minutes randomly after that. Duration ~18 seconds each. Rewards are modest — noticeable but never strong enough to feel mandatory.

Design Constraints

Systems can be complex. Understanding must be immediate and intuitive
Clicking is always optional — reactive, never a grind
No upgrade paths that feel like permanent sacrifice
Passive play is valid but softly capped — extended offline time should not trigger prestige or skip stages
Active play is the intended mode — always faster, always more rewarding
Prestige resets feel like acceleration, not punishment
